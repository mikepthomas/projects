Intro

I am a fan of Look Mum No Computer, in one of his videos he mentions about the website Music From Outer Space and the book Make: Analog Synthesizers which has made me follow the deep (and expensive) rabbit hole of Analog Modular Synthesys.

Controller

I wanted something that I could connect up to a computer via USB to use software synths like VCVRack or for connecting up to Garageband and maybe later Logic Pro. I also wanted something that is able to control hardware synths with Pitch CV, Velocity CV and Gate control or via MIDI inputs and outputs. The Keystep Pro provides all 3, via USB, MIDI and mono 3.5mm output jacks.

DIY Eurorack Case

The case is designed specifically to accommodate the DIY modules from the kit, therefore it does not have threaded screw bar mounts to accomodate any eurorack module and only has screw holes in Horizontal Pitch for modules in a specific order.

The order of the modules are as follows:

  • 10 HP Sequencer
  • 10 HP Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO)
  • 4 HP Wavefolder
  • 10 HP Noise/Sample & Hold (S&H)
  • 8 HP Mixer
  • 10 HP Voltage Controlled Filter (VCF)
  • 8 HP Envelope Generator (EG)
  • 8 HP Voltage Controlled Amplifier (VCA)
  • 8 HP Envelope Generator (EG)
  • 8 HP Output Mixer

Envelope Generator (EG)

The Envelope generator shapes the amplitude of a sound over time the one in this kit is known as an ADSR envelope generator. ADSR stands for Attack, Decay, Sustain, Release. Attack is how fast the sound ramps up, Decay is how fast the sound ramps down, Sustain is how long the sound lasts for and Release is how fast the sound stops.
